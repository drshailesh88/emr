# Clinical Protocols - Quick Reference Card

## Cardiology

### STEMI (ST-Elevation MI)
**First-Line**: Aspirin 325mg chew + Clopidogrel 600mg + Atorvastatin 80mg + Metoprolol + Ramipril + GTN PRN
**Goal**: Door-to-balloon <90 min
**Red Flag**: Chest pain ‚Üí ECG within 10 min

### Heart Failure (HFrEF)
**Quadruple Therapy**: ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan) + Beta-blocker (Carvedilol) + MRA (Spironolactone) + SGLT2i (Dapagliflozin)
**Monitor**: BNP, daily weight, K+

### Atrial Fibrillation
**Rate Control**: Metoprolol or Diltiazem
**Anticoagulation**: If CHA2DS2-VASc ‚â•2 ‚Üí NOAC (Apixaban/Rivaroxaban) preferred over Warfarin
**INR Target** (if warfarin): 2.0-2.5 for Indians

### Hypertension (India-specific)
**Stage 1**: Amlodipine 5mg OD (CCB first-line for Indians)
**Stage 2**: Amlodipine + Telmisartan + Chlorthalidone
**Target**: <140/90 (130/80 if DM/CKD)

## Pediatrics

### Acute Gastroenteritis
**Cornerstone**: ORS (Plan A: 50-100ml after each stool, Plan B: 75ml/kg over 4h)
**India-Specific**: Zinc 20mg OD x 14 days (10mg if <6 months)
**Red Flag**: Severe dehydration, bloody diarrhea + fever
**AVOID**: Antibiotics (unless dysentery), antidiarrheals

### Pneumonia (2m-5y)
**First-Line**: Amoxicillin HIGH-DOSE 80-90 mg/kg/day TDS x 5 days
**Diagnosis**: Fast breathing (RR >40 if 1-5y, >50 if 2-12m)
**Red Flag**: Chest indrawing, SpO2 <92%, unable to drink
**Reassess**: 48 hours (critical!)

### Fever in Children
**First-Line**: Paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose TDS-QID
**CRITICAL**: NEVER Aspirin (Reye syndrome), Avoid NSAIDs if dengue suspected
**Workup**: Always consider malaria/dengue in India (CBC with platelets, peripheral smear)
**Red Flag**: Age <3 months, toxic appearance, fever >5 days

### Dengue (Pediatric)
**Treatment**: Paracetamol ONLY, aggressive hydration (ORS, coconut water)
**Critical Phase**: Days 3-7 (watch for warning signs)
**Monitor**: Platelets daily, Hct q12h
**Warning Signs**: Abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding
**Refer**: Platelets <50k, Hct rise >20%, shock

### Immunization (India NIS)
- **Birth**: BCG, OPV-0, Hep B
- **6 weeks**: DTwP-1, IPV-1, Hep B-1, Hib-1, Rotavirus-1, PCV-1
- **9 months**: MR-1, OPV-2
- **15 months**: MMR-1, Varicella-1, PCV booster

## OB/GYN

### Antenatal Care (Normal Pregnancy)
**Essential Supplements**: Folic acid 5mg OD (T1) ‚Üí IFA 100mg OD (T2/T3) + Calcium 1000mg OD
**Vaccination**: Td (2 doses if not immunized)
**Visits**: Minimum 4 (12w, 20-24w, 28-32w, 36w+), Ideal 8+
**Screening**: DIPSI at 24-28 weeks (75g OGTT, diagnostic if 2h ‚â•140)

### Gestational Diabetes (DIPSI)
**First-Line**: Medical Nutrition Therapy x 2 weeks
**Second-Line**: Insulin (gold standard) if FBS >95 or 2h PP >120
**Alternative**: Metformin (if patient refuses insulin)
**Targets**: FBS <95, 2h PP <120 mg/dL
**Monitor**: Growth scans q4 weeks, NST from 36 weeks

### Preeclampsia
**Diagnosis**: BP ‚â•140/90 + Proteinuria (after 20 weeks)
**Treatment**: Methyldopa 250mg TDS (first-line) or Labetalol or Nifedipine SR
**NEVER**: ACE-I/ARBs (teratogenic)
**Severe**: MgSO4 (Pritchard/Zuspan regimen) for seizure prophylaxis
**Delivery**: If ‚â•37 weeks or severe features

### Postpartum Care
**Breastfeeding**: Exclusive x 6 months, proper latch, on demand (8-12 times/day)
**Supplements**: IFA x 3 months
**Visits**: Week 1, Week 6, 6 months
**Screen**: Edinburgh scale (postpartum depression)
**Red Flag**: Foul lochia, fever, severe perineal pain, bleeding

### PCOS (Rotterdam Criteria)
**Diagnosis**: 2 of 3 (oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on USG)
**First-Line**: Metformin 500mg BD-TDS + COC (if not planning pregnancy)
**Weight Loss**: 5-10% can restore ovulation
**Ovulation Induction**: Clomiphene 50mg OD x 5 days (if trying to conceive)

## Clinical Calculators

### CHA2DS2-VASc (AF Stroke Risk)
- CHF: 1, Hypertension: 1, Age ‚â•75: 2, Diabetes: 1, Stroke/TIA: 2, Vascular disease: 1, Age 65-74: 1, Female: 1
- **Score ‚â•2**: Anticoagulation recommended

### HAS-BLED (Bleeding Risk)
- Hypertension, Abnormal renal/liver, Stroke, Bleeding, Labile INR, Elderly, Drugs/alcohol (1 point each)
- **Score ‚â•3**: High bleeding risk, address modifiable factors

### Framingham (10-Year CVD Risk)
- India adjustment: Multiply by 1.5 for South Asians
- **<10%**: Low risk, lifestyle modification
- **10-20%**: Moderate, consider statin
- **>20%**: High, statin mandatory, target LDL <70

### Pediatric Dosing (Weight-Based)
- **Paracetamol**: 15 mg/kg/dose (max 75 mg/kg/day)
- **Amoxicillin**: 80-90 mg/kg/day √∑ TDS (high-dose India protocol)
- **Ibuprofen**: 10 mg/kg/dose TDS
- **Zinc**: 20mg OD (10mg if <6 months)

### eGFR (Adult, CKD-EPI)
- Use "non-black" for Indian population
- **<60**: CKD Stage 3 or higher
- **<30**: Refer to nephrologist

### Gestational Age & EDD
- **EDD**: LMP + 280 days (Naegele's rule)
- **Trimester**: T1 (<13w), T2 (13-27w), T3 (>27w)
- **Term**: 37-42 weeks

## India-Specific Protocols

### Why Different from Global Guidelines?

1. **Hypertension**: Indians respond better to CCB (Amlodipine) than ACE-I
2. **Warfarin**: Lower INR target (2.0-2.5 vs 2.0-3.0) ‚Üí safer for Indians
3. **CVD Risk**: South Asians have 1.5x higher risk ‚Üí aggressive prevention
4. **GDM**: DIPSI criteria (simpler, one-step 75g OGTT)
5. **Pediatric**: Zinc for diarrhea (WHO + IAP), high-dose amoxicillin
6. **Dengue/Malaria**: Endemic ‚Üí always consider in fever workup
